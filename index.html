Corporations frequently use statistical tools to help assess the opinions of their customers about different products, services and needs. These tools utilize the ability of consumers to rank and differentiate between different offers, even when all of these offers might be attractive. The only inputs necessary are honest opinions and carefully defined outputs. What if we could use the same tools to help us understand what we like in our friends? And what if we could use the output of these rankings to understand the most important drivers of quality in our friendships and work relationships?

FriendRank is a tool that will use your Facebook and/or LinkedIn connections to help you identify what drives your best friendships and colleague relationships. By answering some simple questions to make tradeoffs between friends you can get to the bottom of how you make friends. Do you value geographic proximity or similar education? Are you more likely to be friends with people who share you interest in music, or your interest in sports? Find out what makes you feel friendly with FriendRank.

FAQ

What tools does FriendRank use to help identify drivers of friendship?
FriendRank will use two primary tools in this analysis - Max Diff analysis to understand rankings of your friends, and CHAID analysis to derive meaning from the results. Max Diff analysis is a tool that will show you various subsets of your friends and ask you to determine who your closest and least close friends are from a set. By answering enough of these questions, the tool can create a verifiable and clear ranking of your top friends. CHAID analysis is a decision tree analysis based upon significance testing. It will utilize a variety of information Facebook and LinkedIn store about each of your friends to try to predict the most important drivers of your relative rankings from the Max Diff.
